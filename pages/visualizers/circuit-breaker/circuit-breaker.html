⚡ Circuit Breaker Explained

🔒

CLOSED — Normal Operation

The breaker is closed (think: a physical circuit is complete). All requests pass through to the downstream service normally. Failures are counted in a rolling window. When failures reach the threshold, the breaker trips.

🚫

OPEN — Fail Fast

The breaker is open — the circuit is broken. Every incoming request is immediately rejected without touching the downstream service. This protects an already-struggling service from more load and gives the caller an instant error instead of hanging for a timeout.

🔆

HALF-OPEN — Cautious Probe

After the cooldown expires, exactly one test request is allowed through. If it succeeds, the breaker resets to CLOSED — service recovered. If it fails, the breaker trips back to OPEN and the cooldown restarts.

⚡

Why It Matters at Scale

Without a breaker, every caller hangs waiting for a timeout (often 30s+) on every request to a dead service. With a breaker OPEN, failures return in microseconds, threads are freed, and the cascade stops. Netflix's Hystrix, Resilience4j, and Istio all implement this pattern.
